Discover a little more about our beautiful town

Serra da Mantiqueira

Campos do Jordão is a Brazilian municipality located in the interior of the state of São Paulo, more precisely in the Serra da Mantiqueira. The city is at an altitude of 1,628 meters, making it the highest Brazilian municipality, considering the altitude of the headquarters. Its estimated population, in 2014, was 50,541 inhabitants. 173 km from the city of São Paulo, 350 km from Rio de Janeiro, and 500 km from Belo Horizonte. Its main access route is the Floriano Rodrigues Pinheiro Highway.

Local Agriculture

Local agriculture plays a crucial role in the economy and sustainability of small towns in the interior. In addition to providing fresh, nutritious food for the local population, agriculture also helps to keep the land productive and preserve biodiversity. Strong and successful agriculture is fundamental for the economic development of the city, as the sale of local agricultural products can generate jobs and increase the income of farmers and producers. In addition, local agriculture reduces dependence on imports, which can help stabilize prices and strengthen the local economy.

Handicraft Fair

Fairs are an excellent way to move trade in small towns. They provide an opportunity for local vendors to showcase their products and services to a wide and diverse audience, attracting people from other areas to visit the city. In addition, markets often feature a wide variety of products and services, from food and handicrafts to clothing and accessories, which can increase the range of products available to local consumers. Fairs can also generate temporary jobs for residents and stimulate tourism, which can help boost the local economy.

Geography

Campos do Jordão is the municipality with the highest administrative headquarters in the country, reaching 1,628 meters above sea level, where the city hall is located, and which can vary to more than 2,000 meters in the surroundings of the municipality. It is located in the Serra da Mantiqueira massif, one of the highest mountain ranges in Brazil. It has an area of 289.5 km². It is constantly visited by tourists from all over Brazil and even abroad, who go there to enjoy the winter weather.

Horse Riding

Horse riding is an activity that boosts local tourism in many ways. The presence of horses and the offer of horseback riding can attract tourists in search of adventure and contact with nature. In addition, horseback riding is an activity that can be practiced by people of all ages and skill levels, which can be a powerful tool to boost local tourism, attract visitors and generate jobs and income for the community.

Winter Tourism

With the inauguration of the Boa Vista Palace, in 1964, and the Classical Music Winter Festival, in 1970, the city started to be considered a reference in winter tourism in Brazil, a position that it maintains until today, thanks to its culture, gastronomy, tradition hospitable and its incomparable natural beauties, in addition to the climate, considered the best in the world at the Paris Climatology Congress in 1957.
